# About

## The EASE Laboratory

The Environmental and Social Epigenetics (EASE) laboratory studies how social and environmental exposures shape the epigenome and contribute to brain health and disease. Based at Boston Children's Hospital and affiliated with Harvard Medical School, the lab integrates multiple omics modalities to investigate the molecular mechanisms through which factors such as social isolation, adverse childhood experiences, and cognitive resilience influence Alzheimer's disease risk and progression.

## ROSMAP

The Religious Orders Study and Memory and Aging Project (ROSMAP) refers to two complementary longitudinal cohort studies of aging and Alzheimer's disease conducted by Rush University Medical Center:

- **Religious Orders Study (ROS)**: Started in 1994, enrolling older Catholic nuns, priests, and brothers.
- **Memory and Aging Project (MAP)**: Started in 1997, enrolling older adults from the greater Chicago area.

Participants undergo annual cognitive and clinical evaluations, and all have agreed to organ donation at the time of death. This provides matched antemortem clinical data (cognitive trajectories, clinical diagnoses, lifestyle factors) with postmortem brain tissue, making ROSMAP one of the most deeply phenotyped brain tissue repositories available for Alzheimer's disease research.

The tissue profiled in these guides comes from the **dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)**, corresponding roughly to Brodmann areas 9 and 46. The DLPFC is commonly studied in Alzheimer's research because it supports higher-order cognitive functions (working memory, executive function) that decline over the course of the disease, and it captures a broad spectrum of disease severity within a single cohort.
